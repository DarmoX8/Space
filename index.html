About

Outer space is the expanse that exists beyond Earth and between celestial bodies. It is where everything (all of the planets, stars, galaxies and other objects) is found. On Earth, space begins at 100 km above sea level. This is where Earth's atmosphere is said to stop and outer space begins.
In space, Earth and other planets is in the solar system. The Solar System is the gravitationally bound system of the Sun and the objects that orbit it, either directly or indirectly. Of the objects that orbit the Sun directly, the largest are the eight planets, with the remainder being smaller objects, the dwarf planets and small Solar System bodies.
Planets are the largest objects in the solar system after the Sun. They orbit the Sun in a path shaped like a oval. They also have an atmosphere, or a layer of gases surrounding them. Most of the planets have at least one moon.
The planets in the Solar System are:

  1. Mercury
  2. Venus
  3. Earth
  4. Mars
  5. Jupiter
  6. Saturn
  7. Uranus
  8. Neptune

The Sun is the star at the center of the Solar System. It is a nearly perfect ball of hot plasma, heated to incandescence by nuclear fusion reactions in its core, radiating the energy mainly as visible light, ultraviolet light, and infrared radiation. It is by far the most important source of energy for life on Earth.
