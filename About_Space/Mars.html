Mars

Mars
Mars is a cold desert world. It is half the size of Earth. Like Earth, Mars has seasons, polar ice caps, volcanoes, canyons, and weather. It has a very thin atmosphere made of carbon dioxide, nitrogen, and argon. Mars is a terrestrial planet. It is small and rocky. Mars has an active atmosphere, but the surface of the planet is not active. Its volcanoes are dead.
4th Planet from the Sun.
Specialty: Mars is called the Red Planet because of rusty iron in the ground.
Other Names: Mangal
Rotation: A speed of 14.9129 mph (24 km/s) and 1 day 37 minutes days for a complete rotation.
Revolution: A speed of 53,853 mph (24.077 km/h), and 687 days for a complete revolution.
Moon: 2 Moons
Axis Tilt: 25°
Temperature: -80°F (-62.2222°C)
Gravity: 3.721 m/s2 or 37.7/100 of Earth
Discovered: 1610 by Galileo Galilei
Atmosphere: 95% by volume of carbon dioxide, 2.6% molecular nitrogen, 1.9% argon, 0.16% molecular oxygen, and 0.06% carbon monoxide
Age: 4.603 billion years
Distance from the Sun: 237.25 million km
Radius: 3,389.5 km
Diameter: 6,779 km
Mass: 6.39 x 10^23 kg
Surface Area: 144.8 million km2
