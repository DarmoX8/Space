Moon

Moon
We can see the moon shining in the sky at night. The Moon also spins or rotates on its own axis. The moon completes one revolution in about the same time that it takes to complete a rotation about its axis. The light from the moon is thrown back to the Earth as moonlight. Moonlight takes nearly one light second to travel from the Moon to the Earth.
About The Moon is a heavenly body that orbits around the earth. Therefore, it is known as a natural satellite of the earth. It is the closest neighbour of earth in space. It appears as a dry, grey-coloured ball of rock. The size of the Moon is much smaller than that of the sun. However, it appears of the same size of the Sun because it is much closer to Earth than the Sun. Just like the Earth, Moon is also made up of three layers â€” core, crust and mantle. It does not have its own light. It shines because it reflects the sunlight that falls on its surface. It is the second brightest object in the sky after the Sun. All the planets except Mercury and Venus in our solar system have moons.
Timeline 4.5 billion years ago, A giant object collided with the Earth. After the collision, the debris formed the Moon.
5th century BCE, Babylonian Astronomers were the first to study and mapped the first lunar cycle.
In 1647, The First map was made to show liberation zones of the moon.
In 1959, The Soviet Union's 'Luna Program' was the first to get a man-made object to escape Earth's gravity. It impacted the surface of the Moon.
Surface The surface of the Moon is made of rocks and dust. It has high mountains, volcanoes and impact craters. The craters have been created on the surface of the Moon due to the impact of colliding meteorites.
Conditions There is no air and water on the Moon. So, no life exists there. However, a thin layer of gases is present on the Moon. No air means there is no atmosphere on the Moon. In the absence of atmosphere, the surface of the Moon becomes very hot during the day and very cold at night. The absence of atmosphere also means that there is no protection from the approaching meteorites. This is the reason why there are so many craters on the Moon. The lack of air also means that two people talking on the Moon cannot hear each other. The reason being that sound waves need a medium to travel. That is why the spacesuits of astronauts are fitted with radio sets. When an astronaut speaks, his radio set converts sound waves into radio waves. Radio waves can travel without a medium. After receiving a radio message, the radio set converts radio waves into sound waves. In this way, the astronauts are able to communicate with each other.
