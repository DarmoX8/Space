Planets

All the planets, except the Earth, have been named after Roman and Greek Gods. A planet needs to fulfill 3 essential criteria.

  1. The object should be a heavenly body revolving around the sun.
  2. The object should be spherical in shape.
  3. The area around it's orbit should be clear and should not have any equivalent or a bigger celestial body.
    With the help of its gravity the planet should clear asteroids and other planets out of its way.

Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus and Neptune fulfill these conditions and thus they are planets.
Pluto, Haumea, Make Make, Eris does not fulfill the 3rd criteria but does fulfill the 1st and 2nd criteria. Thus, called dwarf planets.
