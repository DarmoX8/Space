Phases of the Moon

 

The time period of revolution and rotation of the Moon are almost the same as the Earth. Thus, the same side of the Moon is always facing the Earth. The part of the Moon that faces the sun is lit up by the Sun. Different portions of the Moon are illuminated by the Sun at different times during its rotation. These illuminated portions appear to change shape when viewed from the Earth. These changing shapes of the Moon are known as the Phases of the Moon. When the moon is growing, it is called Waxing. When the moon is shrinking, it is called Waning.

New Moon

In this phase, the illuminated side of the Moon is away from the Earth. The side of the Moon facing the Earth receives no sunlight and hence, cannot be seen from the Earth. This phase is called the New Moon.

Crescent Moon

In this phase, a small portion of the illuminated side of the Moon is facing towards the Earth. So, a small portion of the moon can be seen from the Earth. It is called the Crescent Moon. Waxing Crescent when it's growing. Waning Crescent when it's shrinking.

Half Moon

When half portion of the illuminated side of the Moon is facing towards the Earth, we can see a half of the Moon from the Earth. It is called Half Moon. First Quarter when it's growing. Third Quarter when it's shrinking.

Gibbous Moon

When a large portion of the illuminated side of the Moon is facing towards the Earth, we can see a large portion of the Moon from the Earth. It is called the Gibbous Moon. Waxing Gibbous when it's growing. Waning Gibbous when it's shrinking.

Full Moon

When the entire portion of the illuminated side of the Moon faces towards the Earth, we can see the perfectly round Moon. It is called the Full Moon.

The phases of the Moon reverse after the Full Moon. The time period between two full moons is about 29.5 days.
