Satellites

An object that orbits a planet is called satellites. Satellites can be of 2 types depending upon the objects that are orbiting around the planet.
They are:

  • Natural Satellites
  • Artificial Satellites

Natural Satellites

A celestial object that revolves around a planet is called natural satellites. Eg:- Moon revolving the planets, Planets revolving the Sun

Artificial Satellites

A man made object that is placed in the orbit of a planet to revolve around it is called Artificial Satellite. These are launched into the space using rockets.
Artificial satellites are used for:

Navigation

Navigation Satellites are used to provide the accurate location of a place or something. The Global Positioning System (GPS) is a satellite based navigational system which is made up of a network of 24 satellites. It can be used to find out the exact location of a GPS receiver anywhere on the earth.

Weather Satellites

Weather forecasting is done by studying the pictures taken by the weather satellites. Satellite data help forecast the weather in two ways: expert forecasters interpret the images, and numerical weather-prediction models assimilate observations. Image analysis plays an important role in short-term forecasts, those that predict the weather in one to three hours into the future. These satellites are also used to predict natural calamities such as Earthquakes,cyclones and floods.

Communication Satellites

The purpose of communications satellites is to relay the signal around the curve of the Earth allowing communication between widely separated geographical points. Communications satellites use a wide range of radio and microwave frequencies. Communications satellites are used for television, telephone, radio, internet, and military applications. Other Uses

  • Artificial satellites are widely used to study the Earth and other planets.
  • To observe the astronomical bodes present quite far away from Earth

Some Satellites serve only one function while others are designed to perform multiple functions. Special equipments are used in a satellite to protect it from harmful radiations in space.

International Space Station (ISS)

The International Space Station (ISS) is an artificial satellite present in the earth's orbit. It is the third brightest object in the sky. Several experiments are conducted in ISS as it is an ideal place to realise the zero gravity state. Its main construction was in between 1998 and 2011.
