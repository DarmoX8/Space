Sun

Sun
The Sun makes the day bright and warm. Without the Sun, planets, animals, and people would not be able to live. We cannot see the Sun at night. It looks small because it is very far away. The day begins with sunrise and night begins with sunset. The Sun is at the center of the Solar System. All the planets move around it in their own orbits. The Sun is also a star. It is the closest star to the Earth. That is why, it bigger than the other stars. It appears as a big ball of hot, burning gases. In the Sun, hydrogen is continuously changes into helium gas. This process results in the emission of huge amounts of energy in the form of light and heat. In short terms, Hydrogen is converted to Helium, Light and Heat The Sun is responsible for the changes in climate and weather of the Earth. The energy that we get from the Sun is known as solar energy.
Facts The Sun is nearly 4.6 billion years old.
It is nearly spherical shaped.
It is made up of 70% hydrogen, 28% helium, 1.5% Carbon, nitrogen and oxygen and 0.5% Small amounts of other elements such as neon, iron, silicon, magnesium and sulfur.
