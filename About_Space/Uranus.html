Uranus

Uranus
Uranus is made of water, methane, and ammonia fluids above a small rocky center. Its atmosphere is made of hydrogen and helium like Jupiter and Saturn, but it also has methane. The methane makes Uranus blue. Uranus also has faint rings. The inner rings are narrow and dark. The outer rings are brightly colored and easier to see. It's an ice giant. It is mostly made of flowing icy materials above a solid core.
7th Planet from the Sun.
Specialty: Coldest Planet in the Solar System. The only planet rotating on it's side.
Other Names: Arun
Rotation: A speed of 15,233 mph (6.52 km/s) and a 17 hours 14 minutes for a complete rotation.
Revolution: A speed of 24,607 km/h (15,290 mph) and a 84 years for a complete revolution.
Moon: 27 Moons
Axis Tilt: 97.77°
Temperature: -353°F (-213.889°C)
Gravity: 8.87 m/s2 or 88.9/100 of Earth
Discovered: 13 March 1781 by William Herschel
Atmosphere: 82.5% molecular hydrogen, 15.2% helium, 2.3% methane
Age: 4.503 billion years
Distance from the Sun: 2.9505 billion km
Radius: 25,362 km
Diameter: 50,724 km
Mass: 8.681 x 10^25 kg
Surface Area: 8.083 billion km2
Rings: 13 Rings
